Machine Learning for Healthcare @ CMU

Welcome to the Carnegie Mellon's Machine Learning for Healthcare Reading Group for Spring 2019!

If you would like to join the reading group, please Subscribe to the mailing list. If you are interested in leading a discussion this semester, please contact Chirag Nagpal

Focus

We would like to focus on latest research at the intersection of ML and Healthcare from venues like MLHC, NIPS ML4H, JAMIA, AMIA, KDD, NIPS and ICML. Some of the topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Generative and Graphical Models : Unsupervised and Latent Variable Models, Graphical Models for Multimodal Clinical Data
  • Deep Learning in Healthcare: Novel Architectures, Modelling Temporal and Missing Data, Computer Vision assisted Diagnosis
  • Causal Inference: Heterogenous Treatment Effects, Dosage Response Estimation, NNs and Bayesian Nonparametric Apporaches for CI.
  • Interpretable ML
  • Survival Analysis
  • Bias and Fairness in Healthcare Data Science: Under-representation of Demographic Groups, Covariate Shift due to adverse access to Healthcare
  • Impactful Applications: Real world findings and clinical results using ML.

Expectation

Should you sign up for the reading group, we expect you to lead atleast one discussion in the semester based around a paper of your choice, we would strongly prefer you present a full conference paper. Exceptions will only be made for highly-impactful workshop papers or if you are presenting your own research.

Each discussion session would last from 45 mins to 1 hour, out of which the first ~20 mins would be for the primary presenter to walk the group through the paper.

Presenters are free to choose between projecting slides or using a whiteboard.
